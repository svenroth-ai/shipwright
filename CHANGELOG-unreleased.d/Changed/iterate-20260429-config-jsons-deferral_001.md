**`shipwright_*_config.json` migration evaluated and deferred** (commit `5bdf05c`, 2026-04-29). The compliance Sub-Iterate G follow-up flagged the root-level `shipwright_*_config.json` family (10 files: run/project/plan/build/test/security/compliance/design/deploy/changelog/sync) plus `shipwright_events.jsonl` and `.shipwright_toolcall_count` as a candidate for a fifth `.shipwright/<artefact>/` migration. Three parallel Explore audits (CLI/script coupling, gitignore/tracking semantics, naming-convention coupling) returned ~582 Python hits across ~132 files — the largest migration scope to date — and confirmed zero external coupling (no CI, no S3/upload, no audit-system, no IDE-extension). Senior-architect framing: per-tool configs at root with a clear namespace prefix (`shipwright_*`) match the dominant ecosystem pattern (`tsconfig.json`, `pyproject.toml`, `Cargo.toml`, `playwright.config.ts` etc.) — the right rule is **"high-volume output/cache in dotdir, configs at root with prefix"**, which Shipwright already follows. **Decision: deferred (not rejected).** `docs/migrations/artifact-migration-reference.md` § 0 "Open question" subsection replaced with a new "Deferred migration candidates" subsection parallel to "Rejected migration candidates". Memo `project_config_jsons_migration_deferred.md` documents the analysis and three explicit reactivation triggers: (1) `resolve_project_root()` refactor needed for unrelated reason, (2) external coupling materializes (audit-upload / log-collector with fixed canonical path requirement), (3) WebUI builds a Shipwright-artefact Datei-Browser. Until one of those fires, the deferral stands. Distinct from `e2e/` and `CHANGELOG-unreleased.d/` permanent-rejection memos: those failed industry-convention / external-coupling / sibling-pattern criteria; configs do not.
